Grissom's right-hand man is Jack Napier (Jack Nicholson), a brutal man who is not entirely sane... After falling out between the two Grissom has Napier set up with the Police and Napier falls to his apparent death in a vat of chemicals. However, he soon reappears as The Joker and starts a reign of terror in Gotham City. Meanwhile, reporter Vicki Vale (Kim Basinger) is in the city to do an article on Batman. She soon starts a relationship with Batman's everyday persona, billionaire Bruce Wayne.</span>

     <div id="alternateVersions"  class="txt-block">
        <h4>Alternate Versions</h4>
An unofficial version of the film has aired on Latin American TV at least once. Besides being dubbed into Spanish, the film&#39;s ending is heavily edited as follows: When The Joker puts on glasses and says: &quot;You wouldn&#39;t hit a guy with glasses on, would you?&quot; Batman punches him and knocks him over the edge. In the original version the climax continues beyond this point. But in this changed version, The Joker simply plummets to his death after the punch. This was achieved by cutting from the punch to the birds-eye-view shot of The Joker falling. The next shot is simply The Joker lying dead and the crowd of people looming over him. The entire struggle on the ledge and attempted helicopter escape are completely omitted.        <a href="alternateversions?tab=cz&ref_=tt_trv_alt"

                <span>
                    <strong>Stylish and Thrilling</strong>
                    <div class="comment-meta">
                        7 June 2006 | by <a href="/user/ur3924830/?ref_=tt_urv"
><span>ReelCheese</span></a>
                        &ndash; <a href="/user/ur3924830/comments?ref_=tt_urv"
>See all my reviews</a>
                    </div>
                    <div>
                        <p>Dark, foreboding and vastly entertaining, BATMAN changed superhero movies forever. Gone are the days of happy-go-lucky heroes arriving just in time to save the day. Director Tim Burton&#39;s Caped Crusader is a vengeful creature of the night preying on criminals who would turn his city into their personal playground.<br/><br/>BATMAN is one stylish, thrilling and one helluva ride. While he&#39;s not generally associated with action, Burton proves it to be his forte as the Dark Knight Detective swoops out of the shadows, crashes through windows and employs a host of what his nemesis calls &quot;wonderful toys,&quot; such as grappling hook guns and the amazingly versatile Batmobile. And man on man, this Batman kicks some ass, at one point daring an armed henchman with a &quot;come here&quot; finger gesture.<br/><br/>BATMAN wouldn&#39;t have worked nearly as well as it does without the right casting ingredients. The hiring of Michael Keaton in the lead role drew plenty of snickers, but the underrated actor showed up his detractors with a suitably mysterious performance. We know his heart is in the right place, but we&#39;re never quite sure what we should make of him. Jack Nicholson&#39;s famous performance as the villainous Joker is every bit as good as they say. Nicholson is equal parts amusing and sadistic beneath the white face paint and green hair. Sexy Kim Basinger, as reporter Vicki Vale, also makes the most out of her screen time.<br/><br/>BATMAN is one of those films whose obvious flaws are so easily overlooked. The worst sin a movie can commit is to be dull, and that&#39;s something BATMAN never does.</p>
