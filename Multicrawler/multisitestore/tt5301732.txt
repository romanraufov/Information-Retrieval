                        <p>Some of the more negative reviews seem to be written by those who take their TV very seriously. I thought the return was a great mix of weird, scary, funny, mysterious, apocalyptic and crazy. Come to think of it, that&#39;s what I liked about the original series, too. So now the characters are older, did we expect them to be 20-somethings? It&#39;s not the 90s anymore, so the impending doom of the millennium has been and gone. Now the darkness is different. It&#39;s bigger. The new young characters are perfectly in tone with the whole charisma of the series: ultra-serious, naive, smart, unintentionally funny (thanks to the writers and actors, it doesn&#39;t seem labored), sometimes out-of-control, etc. People wanting to see the original series should buy the DVDs. Me, I&#39;m older too, and I like the new series. I hope it sticks around, and that people can lighten up a bit on the hard-core expectations that it stick to what it &quot;should&quot; be. Thankfully, though, the subtle, self-mocking humor that has always been a part of the series hasn&#39;t gone anywhere.<br/><br/>I agree with the person who wondered what the heck is wrong with Fox&#39;s scheduling, though. It made no sense at all, unless they were trying to kill the series before it started.</p>
